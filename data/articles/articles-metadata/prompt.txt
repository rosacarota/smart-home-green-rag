You are preparing one article for a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) knowledge base about green smart-home trigger-action rules.

The final goal of the knowledge base is to help an LLM transform existing smart-home IF-THEN automation rules into greener versions while preserving the user's original functional intent, comfort, and safety.

TASK
Read the provided article carefully and extract ONLY document-level information, without creating chunks or patterns.

You must:
1. Understand whether and how the article is relevant to green smart-home automation and trigger-action rules.
2. Produce a concise article summary focused on aspects that could conceptually support greener smart-home rules.
3. Assign binary one-hot values (0/1) for the following normalized green categories, based on the overall content of the article.

Use ONLY the following normalized green categories as one-hot fields:

- hvac_optimization
- lighting_efficiency
- water_saving
- standby_reduction
- appliance_energy_management
- energy_monitoring
- occupancy_based_control
- environmental_awareness
- scheduling_optimization

INTERPRETATION RULES FOR CATEGORIES
Set a category to 1 if the article contains content that is:
- directly focused on that topic, OR
- provides concepts, findings, or design ideas that can reasonably inform greener rules for that topic.

Otherwise, set it to 0.

Guidelines per category:

- hvac_optimization: 1 if the article discusses heating/cooling management, thermal comfort, thermostats, HVAC control, energy-aware temperature management, or scheduling of heating/cooling in homes.

- lighting_efficiency: 1 if the article discusses lighting control, lights automation, using daylight, dimming, turning lights off when not needed, or energy-efficient lighting behavior.

- water_saving: 1 if the article discusses water consumption, irrigation, smart watering, or any strategy explicitly related to reducing water waste.

- standby_reduction: 1 if the article discusses standby consumption, always-on devices, avoiding unnecessary idle power, or similar ideas.

- appliance_energy_management: 1 if the article discusses appliance usage (washing machines, dishwashers, dryers, fridges, ovens, etc.), scheduling or optimizing their use, or shifting their operation to greener/cheaper conditions.

- energy_monitoring: 1 if the article discusses monitoring or visualizing energy consumption, feedback dashboards, digital twins that show energy usage, ecological footprint displays, or any analytics/monitoring features related to energy. (For example, visualizing consumption, CO2 equivalent, or forecasts for energy demand in a smart home.)

- occupancy_based_control: 1 if the article discusses presence detection, occupants’ behavior, adapting automations to when people are home/away, or tailoring control to users’ activities and routines.

- environmental_awareness: 1 if the article discusses sustainability awareness, ecological footprint, behavior change for sustainability, climate impact, or educational/gamification mechanisms to increase environmental consciousness.

- scheduling_optimization: 1 if the article discusses when automations run (timing), off-peak usage, shifting loads in time, optimized schedules for devices, cost-aware or grid-aware timing, or similar concepts.

IMPORTANT:
- An article can activate multiple categories at once (set multiple values to 1).
- If a topic is mentioned only in a very generic way, but still clearly relevant to one of the categories above, you can set that category to 1.
- Do NOT activate a category if there is no clear connection in the article text.

OUTPUT FORMAT
Return ONLY valid JSON with this exact structure:

{
  "article_id": "ARTICLE_001",
  "source_title": "",

  "normalized_green_categories_one_hot": {

FILLING RULES
- article_id: keep the ID provided by the caller, or "ARTICLE_001" if none is given.
- source_title: the title of the article, if clearly available in the text; otherwise leave as empty string.
- year: the publication year if clearly detectable; otherwise leave empty.
- article_summary: write 2–4 sentences that summarize ONLY the aspects of the article that are relevant to green smart-home behavior, sustainability, automation, user behavior, energy awareness, or design concepts that could inform greener rules. Do not summarize unrelated parts.
- normalized_green_categories_one_hot: set each field to 0 or 1 following the interpretation rules above.

GENERAL CONSTRAINTS
